# Employee Performance & Productivity Analysis

This project analyzes employee performance and productivity using a large-scale dataset of 100,000 employees.  
The goal is to identify key drivers of performance and provide data-driven recommendations for workforce optimization.

## Overview
- Dataset size: 100,000 employees  
- Average salary: ~$6,400  
- Performance distribution:
  - Low: ~40%
  - Medium: ~40%
  - High: ~20%  


##  Objectives
- Evaluate overall employee performance  
- Identify factors affecting productivity  
- Provide insights to improve workforce efficiency  
- Support HR decision-making  



## Key Insights
- **Salary is the strongest driver of performance**  
  → Higher salary consistently leads to higher performance regardless of satisfaction  

- **Hybrid work model (25–50%) achieves optimal productivity**  
  → Fully remote or fully onsite is less effective  

- **Moderate overtime improves performance, excessive overtime reduces it**  
  → Overworking leads to diminishing returns  

- **Training quantity has no significant impact on performance**  
  → Training effectiveness matters more than volume  

- **Promotion does not guarantee higher performance**  
  → Employees may need time to adapt or are promoted based on other factors  

- **Attrition is not strongly related to performance**  
  → Retention is influenced by other factors (e.g., compensation, environment)  


## Business Impact
The analysis highlights that employee performance is driven mainly by compensation and work environment rather than demographic characteristics.

Organizations can improve productivity by:
- Optimizing salary strategy  
- Adopting flexible work models  
- Managing workload effectively  
- Redesigning training programs  



## Recommendations
- Increase salary competitiveness to improve performance  
- Implement hybrid work model for optimal productivity  
- Avoid excessive overtime and maintain workload balance  
- Focus on training quality instead of quantity  
- Reevaluate promotion criteria beyond performance
